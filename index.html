Our Culture

Any millennial who has witnessed the evolution of modern music, cannot help but recognize the explosion in popularity of Electronic Dance Music (EDM) in recent years. “Raves” or music festivals such as EDC and Ultra attract crowds of the hopeful dancing youth, dressed in garish freakadelic clothes, by the thousands. In a way, this music branding has shaped its own counter-culture, granting new modes of self-expression and freedom for the youth who seek to be liberated within the crowds of these shows. In the simplest sense, the clothes ravers wear are symbolic of their fixation on fantasy and stepping out of everyday life.

One of these popular clothing items are bandanas. Bandanas have long served as a stylish rave accessory, being worn as facemasks and bracelets. They also serve much more practical purposes such as hair ties and most importantly protection for dusty EDM festival grounds. But two of the dangers of wearing these masks for long periods of time are: overheating and the cutting off of circulation of air due to the obstruction that cloth causes. These lead to serious health risks that have not been fixed for decades.
